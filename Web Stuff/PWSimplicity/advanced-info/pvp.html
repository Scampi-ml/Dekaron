Player vs. Player

Within the game several modes of battle exist. They range from Player vs. Environment (PVE) which includes monsters, bosses, and raids to Player vs. Player (PVP). In PVP players actively engage each other in combat in order to test skills and kill one another. When you kill another player this is known as player killing or PKing. Below are the different visual character statuses a player can be in:

Blue Names: Player is in shield mode; they cannot attack or be attacked by any players.

White Names: Player is currently neutral. They may have activated PK mode but has yet to attack anyone.

Pink Names: Player has definitely killed other players. In this mode you have a chance to lose items from your inventory and your equipment slots if you are killed while infamous, so be careful!

Red name: Player has killed many other players. Be careful of red names because you can be next. The more kills you rack up the longer you will stay in this mode. The punishment for death while you have a red name is far more severe than usual. Killing monsters at or above your level will reduce the duration of being infamous.

Note: There are lots of safe zones in Perfect World. You can't PK in these areas.

Note: Players are forced into PK status during Territory Wars and some special activities. There is no death penalty during these times.

Players of different levels and server types will have different PK restrictions. They are as follows:

PvP Server

Level 1-29: Between these levels the users are not permitted to enter PK mode. They cannot attack or be attacked by other players.

Level 30 and higher: These levels are free to PK. Once users become this level they are flagged for PVP. There is no way to turn it off but areas like cities will still be considered safe zones and will be PK off.

PvE Server

Level 1-29: Between these levels the users are not permitted to enter PK mode. They cannot attack or be attacked by other players.

Level 30 and higher: These levels are free to PK but in order to do so the player must activate it themselves. In this way a user will not have to PK if they do not want to unless they enter areas that require PK like territory wars.
